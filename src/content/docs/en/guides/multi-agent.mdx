## One-sentence explanation

Multi-agent design splits complex work across specialist agents.

## Analogy

A medical paper is not written by one person in isolation. You may need a literature advisor, a statistics advisor, and a writing advisor.

## Minimal code

```python
from agents import Agent, Runner

literature_agent = Agent(name="Literature Assistant", instructions="Only provide search strategy and evidence notes.")
statistics_agent = Agent(name="Statistics Assistant", instructions="Only provide study design and statistical advice.")

orchestrator = Agent(
    name="Medical Research Orchestrator",
    instructions="Call specialist tools and integrate a concise research draft.",
    tools=[
        literature_agent.as_tool(
            tool_name="literature_helper",
            tool_description="Provide literature search strategy and evidence signals.",
        ),
        statistics_agent.as_tool(
            tool_name="statistics_helper",
            tool_description="Provide study design and statistical analysis suggestions.",
        ),
    ],
)

result = Runner.run_sync(orchestrator, "Design a retrospective ICU sepsis study.")
print(result.final_output)
```

## Medical research use

Prefer a main agent that owns the final answer and calls specialist agents as tools. That keeps safety boundaries, output format, and voice consistent.

## Common mistakes

- Every specialist becomes a general assistant.
- Too many agents increase cost and debugging effort.
- No orchestrator means users receive fragmented answers.
